## Brand Voice Guide

### Tone Attributes
1. **Confident** — Declarative statements. No hedging with "we believe" or "we think." State it.
2. **Direct** — Short sentences. Active voice. Get to the point in the first line.
3. **Measured** — Claims backed by structure, specifics, or numbers. No superlatives without substance.
4. **Human** — Written for executives, not algorithms. Warm enough to trust, sharp enough to respect.
5. **Outcome-anchored** — Every paragraph ties back to a business result. No feature without a payoff.

### Voice Do's
- Lead with the business outcome, then explain the mechanism
- Use numbers when accurate ("47% reduction" not "significant improvement")
- Name the pain directly ("Your ops team is duct-taping workflows" not "Many businesses face challenges")
- Write at a CEO reading level — clear, decisive, zero filler
- Use "you/your" to address the reader; use "we" sparingly and only when describing Blackwell's actions
- Contractions are fine — "you'll," "we're," "it's" — they sound human
- Frame AI as the tool, business judgment as the product

### Voice Don'ts
- No buzzwords: "innovative," "cutting-edge," "leverage," "synergy," "disrupt," "unlock"
- No hedging: "We believe," "We strive to," "We aim to"
- No hype: "Revolutionary AI," "Transform everything," "The future is here"
- No jargon without context: "LLM orchestration" is fine in a blog post, not on the homepage hero
- No passive voice in headlines or CTAs
- No "We build websites" framing — ever
- No generic stock-photo language ("In today's fast-paced world...")

### Example Phrases
- ✅ "Your AI maturity score tells you exactly where to invest next."
- ✅ "We assessed 14 workflows and automated the 6 that were costing you $180K/year."
- ✅ "Strategy calls are 30 minutes. No pitch deck. Just diagnosis."
- ❌ "We leverage cutting-edge AI to deliver innovative solutions."
- ❌ "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape..."
- ❌ "We believe in the power of transformation."

## CTA Strategy

### Primary CTA

**Text:** Take the QuickScan
**Destination:** `/quickscan`
**Style:** Filled accent button — highest visual weight
**Placement:**
- Header (always visible, desktop and mobile)
- Homepage hero (above-the-fold)
- Homepage final CTA section
- Every service page (secondary option alongside "Book a Strategy Call")
- Framework page (embedded mid-content and final section)
- Blog posts (inline mid-article CTA)
- Mobile sticky bottom bar (scroll-triggered, all pages except /quickscan and /contact)
- Desktop floating CTA (after 60% scroll on long-form pages)
- 404 page
- Footer (button style)

**Rationale:**
The QuickScan is the central conversion engine. It qualifies leads, segments them by service tier, captures contact information, and delivers immediate value — all before a human conversation. Every page should provide a clear path to the QuickScan for visitors who aren't ready to book a call.

**Frequency:**
Minimum 2 exposure points per page (hero/intro + closing CTA). Long-form pages (Framework, Services detail, Blog posts) include a third mid-content exposure. Never more than 3 per page to avoid fatigue.

---

### Secondary CTA

**Text:** Book a Strategy Call
**Destination:** `/contact`
**Style:** Ghost/outline button or text link — lower visual weight than primary CTA
**Placement:**
- Header (text link beside primary CTA, desktop only)
- Homepage hero (secondary button)
- Homepage final CTA section (secondary button)
- Every service page (primary CTA on service pages, since these visitors are further in the funnel)
- Results page (primary on results, secondary on other pages)
- About page
- Footer (text link)

**Rationale:**
For visitors who already know their problem, trust the brand, or have taken the QuickScan — a direct call booking is the shortest path to engagement. The strategy call CTA should always be available but never compete with the QuickScan for attention.
